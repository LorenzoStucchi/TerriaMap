About MARINEWIND

MARINEWIND project is a 36 months Coordination and Support Action (CSA) with the primary objective of identifying challenges and potential opportunities to enhance the role of Floating Offshore Wind Technology (FOWT) in innovative system integration solutions. The project explores various aspects such as market dinamics, policy and regulatory considerations (WP1), social and environmental factors (WP2), optimal financial and techno-economic solutions, and suggestions for storage and flexibility (WP3).

The MARINEWIND methodology is based on the analysis of the MARINEWIND Labs, pilot studies located in five countries: Portugal and UK (current applications of FOWT) and Greece, Spain and Italy (planned applications of FOWT). These Labs will support the transfer of knowledge from more established experiences to the potential FOWT sites where plans for installation of FOWT are still at various stages of planning or implementation. The MARINEWIND Labs involve the Quintuple Helix stakeholders (industry,academia, public authorities, civil society and green innovation).

The MARINEWIND Web-based Geographical Information System (webGIS) will provide specific information on FOWTs to various stakeholders on the basis of their category, geographical location and their goals and policy recommendations on how to have more informed RES policy and how to increase societal acceptance.

The data catalogue organizes resources by the five partner countries, covering administrative boundaries, marine cartography, Floating Offshore Wind (FOW) projects, protected areas, network infrastructure and outcomes from co-creation workshops. Additionally, European-scale data is available, offering environmental insights and maritime use information relevant for FOW development.

Each data is completed with a brief description, source and identification of potential key stakeholders.

Users can overlay data on an interactive map with functionalities for visualization, navigation and querying. Custom external data can also be imported to be visualized locally on the map and compared or integrated with marinewind available data within that specific web session.

An “Additional Tool” section enables preliminary techno-economic assessments of potential offshore wind sites located in a pre-selected point on the map, simulating energy cost through two methodologies (WORK IN PROGRESS).
