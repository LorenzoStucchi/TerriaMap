About

Integrated Atlas for the national energy system is a WebGIS tool to support energy planning. The aim of the Integrated Atlas is to provide a picture as complete as possible, on the whole national territory, by integrating data and information relating to the various elements of the energy sector (renewable resources, demands, territorial restrictions, energy production plants, etc.) from a system perspective.

The Integrated Atlas has functions that can be defined as basic (typical of WebGIS products), and advanced functions to support the modeling of multi-energy systems (TOTEM, energy characterization tool of the territory at the provincial level).

Offering itself as a tool to support energy planning and hosting various descriptive elements of the energy sector and the territory, the spatial resolutions of the data contained are also different: ranging from the regional and provincial level (e.g.: reprocessing of statistical data relating to electricity consumption and sectoral electricity consumption), to some details at the municipal level (e.g.: photovoltaic, wind, geothermal plants),to data at the single plant level (e.g.: hydroelectric reservoirs), to detailed resolutions of the wind power generation maps and annual solar radiation.

All the data presented (even those derived from statistical data) are provided for study and research purposes and do not in any way replace the statistical data produced by the appointed subjects, within the SISTAN (e.g.: ISTAT, GSE, TERNA ).
