About Aeolian

The impetus that energy policies with their ambitious targets for the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions for 2030 and 2050 are giving to the development of the wind sector, combined with the growing attention towards offshore wind, makes it particularly urgent to have accurate and reliable data on the availability and distribution of wind resources in the national territory, both to allow correct territorial planning and as a support tool for the implementation of effective and sustainable interventions.

The goal of AEOLIAN is, therefore, to provide an anemological database on the Italian territory that can be consulted through an easily usable WebGIS tool in order to constitute a tangible support to energy planning for various stakeholders, such as wind farm builders, authorities responsible for territorial planning, companies involved in the development of the electricity grid or even private citizens.

AEOLIAN has been developed to provide information on the general distribution of wind resources in Italy and to help identify areas where these resources may be of interest for energy exploitation.
However, it should be noted that the contents of the Atlas cannot and do not intend to replace the detailed evaluation framework (micrositing) that anyone intending to build a wind farm for the production of electricity must always and in any case acquire at the candidate sites in order to address them from an anemological point of view.

All the data presented (even those derived from statistical data) are provided for study and research purposes and do not in any way replace the data produced by the appointed subjects (eg: GSE, ANEV).

Methodology

The new AEOLIAN Italian Wind Atlas provides an updated anemological database consisting of 30 years (1990-2019) of hourly wind data with a horizontal resolution of 1.4 km covering the entire Italian territory and marine areas, estimated through the meteorological model Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) combined with a statistical post-processing based on Analog Ensemble (AnEn).
Initially, dynamic simulations were performed at an hourly rate for the period 1990-2019 with a horizontal resolution of 4 km. For the period 2015-2019, a nesting with an internal grid of 1.33 km was subsequently carried out, subsequently using the AnEn to extend the 5-year high-resolution processing back in time to 1990, in order to create a 30-year high-resolution dataset covering all of Italy and the surrounding marine areas. The final resolution of the dataset following the reprojection on this WebGIS is equal to 1.4 km in the WGS84 UTM32 reference system.

These calculations are the result of a collaboration between Ricerca sul Sistema Energetico (RSE) SpA and the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR).

For further information, please refer to the System Research report «Il Nuovo Atlante Eolico Italiano: sviluppo e guida d’uso per l’utente».

The historical data relating to the anemometric stations can currently be consulted by the Integrated Atlas and downloadable from GeoDB ETA.
